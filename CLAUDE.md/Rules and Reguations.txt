## 🇮🇪 Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
Since Ireland is in the EU, GDPR is strictly enforced — and Ireland's Data Protection Commission (DPC) is one of the most active regulators in Europe (it oversees Meta, Google, etc.).

- You **must have a Privacy Policy** if you collect any personal data (name, email, IP address, etc.)
- You need a **Cookie Consent banner** — users must opt *in* before non-essential cookies are set (pre-ticked boxes are illegal)
- You must state your **lawful basis** for processing data (consent, legitimate interest, contract, etc.)
- Users have the right to **access, delete, and port** their data — you must be able to honour these requests
- If you collect data from EU citizens, you must appoint a **Data Controller** and potentially a **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
- Data breaches must be reported to the DPC within **72 hours**
- Register with the DPC if required: **dataprotection.ie**

### ePrivacy Regulations (Cookie Law)
- Cookies that are **strictly necessary** (login sessions, shopping carts) don't need consent
- **Analytics, marketing, and tracking cookies** require explicit opt-in consent
- You must provide an easy way to **withdraw consent** at any time

### Consumer Rights & E-Commerce (if selling online)
- Under the **EU Consumer Rights Directive**, you must display: full business name, address, contact details, and VAT number
- Clear **terms & conditions** and **returns policy** are required
- You must show the **total price** including VAT and any fees before checkout
- Customers have a **14-day right to cancel** (cooling-off period) for most goods/services bought online
- Comply with the **Consumer Rights Act 2022** (Ireland's implementation of EU rules)

### Accessibility (EN 301 549 / WCAG 2.1)
- **Public sector websites** in Ireland are legally required to meet WCAG 2.1 AA under the **European Accessibility Act (EAA)**
- The EAA comes into full force for **private sector** businesses in **June 2025** — this applies to e-commerce, banking, transport, and digital services
- Key requirements: keyboard navigation, sufficient colour contrast, alt text for images, captions for video, screen reader compatibility

### Intellectual Property
- Don't use images, fonts, or code without proper licences
- If you use open-source code, check the licence terms (MIT, GPL, etc.)
- Protect your own brand — consider trademarking your name/logo through the **Intellectual Property Office of Ireland (IPOI)**

### Business Registration
- If operating commercially, you should be registered with the **Companies Registration Office (CRO)** or as a sole trader with Revenue
- Display your **registered business name and address** on the site
- Include your **VAT number** if VAT registered (required if turnover exceeds €37,500 for services or €75,000 for goods)

### Defamation & Content Law
- Ireland has strict defamation laws — be careful with user-generated content, reviews, and any claims about competitors
- The **Defamation Act 2009** applies online as well as in print

- Use **HTTPS / SSL certificate** — mandatory for trust, SEO, and legally required if handling personal data under GDPR
- Implement a **Content Security Policy (CSP)** header
- Keep software, plugins, and CMS platforms updated
- Use **strong password policies** and 2FA for admin access

- Alternatively, use **.com**, **.eu**, etc.
- For GDPR compliance, hosting within the **EU/EEA** is strongly recommended (data transfer rules apply if hosting in the US)

## 📈 SEO (Search Engine Optimisation)

### Technical SEO
- Use **HTTPS** (Google ranks secure sites higher)
- Ensure your site is **mobile-friendly** (Google uses mobile-first indexing)
- Fast **page load speed** — aim for Core Web Vitals passing scores (LCP, FID/INP, CLS)
- Submit a **sitemap.xml** to Google Search Console
- Use a **robots.txt** file correctly
- Implement **structured data / schema markup** (LocalBusiness, Product, FAQ, etc.)
- Fix **broken links** and avoid duplicate content
- Use **canonical tags** to avoid duplicate page issues
- Ensure proper **URL structure** (clean, descriptive URLs)

### On-Page SEO
- Each page needs a unique **title tag** (50–60 characters) and **meta description** (150–160 characters)
- Use **H1, H2, H3 headings** in a logical hierarchy
- Write high-quality, original content — Google penalises thin or copied content
- Optimise **images** (descriptive file names, alt text, compressed file sizes)
- Use **internal linking** between relevant pages

### Local SEO (Very important for Irish businesses)
- Create and verify a **Google Business Profile** (maps listing)
- Use **local keywords** (e.g. "plumber Dublin", "accountant Cork")
- Get listed on **Irish directories**: Golden Pages, Yelp IE, Bing Places, etc.
- Ensure your **NAP (Name, Address, Phone)** is consistent everywhere online
- Encourage **Google reviews** from customers
- Add **LocalBusiness schema markup** to your site

### Irish-Specific SEO Tips
- Target **Irish spelling and terminology** where relevant
- Use **hreflang tags** if you serve both Irish and international audiences
- Consider creating content around **Irish events, holidays** (St. Patrick's Day, bank holidays) for seasonal traffic
- Register with **Google Search Console** and **Bing Webmaster Tools**

## 1. GDPR — The Full Picture

### What Counts as Personal Data
Almost everything: names, email addresses, phone numbers, IP addresses, cookie IDs, location data, photos, payment info, device identifiers, and even behavioural data like browsing habits. If it can identify a person — directly or indirectly — it's personal data.

### The 7 Principles You Must Follow (Article 5 GDPR)
1. **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** — tell people what you're doing with their data
2. **Purpose limitation** — only collect data for a specific, stated reason; don't reuse it for something else
3. **Data minimisation** — only collect what you actually need
4. **Accuracy** — keep data up to date
5. **Storage limitation** — don't keep data longer than necessary; have a retention policy
6. **Integrity & confidentiality** — keep data secure
7. **Accountability** — you must be able to *prove* you comply

### Lawful Bases for Processing (You Must Choose One)
- **Consent** — freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous opt-in (no pre-ticked boxes)
- **Contract** — processing is necessary to fulfil a contract (e.g. an order)
- **Legal obligation** — you're legally required to process it (e.g. tax records)
- **Vital interests** — protecting someone's life
- **Public task** — for public authorities
- **Legitimate interests** — you have a genuine business need, but this requires a balancing test and can be overridden by the individual's rights

### Privacy Policy — What It Must Contain
- Who you are (name, address, contact)
- What data you collect and why
- Your lawful basis for each type of processing
- Who you share data with (third parties, processors)
- How long you keep data
- Whether you transfer data outside the EU (and what safeguards are in place)
- All 8 individual rights (see below)
- How to complain to the DPC
- Cookie information
- Date of last update

### The 8 Individual Rights You Must Honour
1. **Right to be informed** — your privacy policy covers this
2. **Right of access** — respond to Subject Access Requests (SARs) within **1 month**
3. **Right to rectification** — correct inaccurate data within 1 month
4. **Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")** — delete data on request (with some exceptions)
5. **Right to restrict processing** — limit how you use their data
6. **Right to data portability** — provide their data in a machine-readable format
7. **Right to object** — especially to direct marketing; you must stop immediately
8. **Rights related to automated decision-making** — if you use profiling or automated decisions

### Data Breach Protocol
- Assess the breach immediately
- If it risks people's rights and freedoms, report to the **DPC within 72 hours** — even if you don't have all the details yet
- If it's *high risk*, you must also **notify the affected individuals directly**
- Keep an internal record of ALL breaches, even minor ones
- DPC reporting: **forms.dataprotection.ie**

### Data Processors vs. Controllers
- **You are the Controller** — you decide why and how data is processed
- **Third-party services are Processors** — email providers (Mailchimp), analytics (Google), hosting companies, payment providers, CRMs
- You must have a **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** with every processor
- Most reputable services (Google, Stripe, etc.) provide these automatically — check their terms

### When You Need a Data Protection Officer (DPO)
- You're a public authority
- You carry out **large-scale systematic monitoring** of individuals
- You process **special category data** at large scale (health, religion, ethnicity, sexual orientation, biometric data, political opinions)
- If in doubt, appoint one anyway — it's good practice

### Special Category Data (Extra Restrictions)
This requires **explicit consent** or another specific condition:
- Health/medical data
- Biometric data
- Genetic data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Sexual orientation or sex life

### International Data Transfers
If your hosting, analytics, or any service sends data outside the EU/EEA:
- The destination country must have an **adequacy decision** (e.g. UK currently has one, USA does not by default)
- Or you need **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**
- Or **Binding Corporate Rules** (for large multinationals)
- This is particularly relevant with **Google Analytics** — Irish DPC has flagged this as a compliance issue

## 2. Cookies & ePrivacy — Full Breakdown

### Cookie Categories
| Type | Examples | Consent Required? |
|---|---|---|
| Strictly necessary | Login sessions, shopping cart, security | ❌ No |
| Functional/Preference | Language, region, UI settings | ✅ Yes |
| Analytics | Google Analytics, Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity | ✅ Yes |
| Marketing/Advertising | Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, remarketing | ✅ Yes |
| Social media | Facebook Like buttons, embedded tweets | ✅ Yes |

### Your Cookie Banner Must
- Appear **before** any non-essential cookies are set
- Have a clear **Accept** and **Reject** option (equal prominence — reject cannot be hidden)
- Not use dark patterns (e.g. green accept / grey reject, or burying the reject button)
- Allow users to manage preferences by category
- Let users **withdraw consent** as easily as they gave it
- Remember consent — don't ask again on every visit (store consent record)
- Be logged — keep a record of when and how consent was given

### Cookie Policy Page
- List every cookie on your site by name
- State its purpose, type, and duration
- Name the provider (first party or third party)
- Update it when you add new cookies

### Google Analytics & GDPR in Ireland
The DPC has been particularly active here. To use GA4 legally:
- Only fire GA after consent is given
- Enable **IP anonymisation**
- Turn off **data sharing** with Google's other products
- Consider **Google Consent Mode v2** for proper integration with your cookie banner
- Alternatively, use a privacy-friendly alternative: **Matomo** (self-hosted), **Plausible** (Irish/EU hosted option), or **Fathom**

## 3. E-Commerce Law — Complete Requirements

### Information You Must Display (EU Directive 2011/83)
Before a customer places an order you must clearly show:
- Your full legal business name
- Physical address (a PO box is not sufficient)
- Email address
- Phone number (if you have one)
- VAT number (if registered)
- Company registration number
- Total price including all taxes and fees
- Delivery costs and timescales
- Payment methods accepted
- Your returns and cancellation policy
- Details of any digital content (file formats, DRM, compatibility)

### The 14-Day Cooling-Off Period
- Customers can cancel most online purchases within **14 days** of receiving goods, for **any reason**
- You must refund within **14 days** of receiving the return
- You cannot charge for the return of faulty goods
- **Exceptions**: personalised items, perishables, digital content once downloaded/streamed (if they consented to waive the right), sealed hygiene products

### Digital Services Act (DSA) — New EU Law
Came into full effect in 2024 for most platforms:
- If you host user-generated content (comments, reviews, listings), you must have a content **reporting and moderation system**
- You must act on illegal content reports
- Larger platforms have additional obligations (transparency reports, etc.)
- Most small Irish websites only need basic complaint/reporting mechanisms

### Payment Processing Requirements
- Never store raw card details on your server — use a compliant payment processor (Stripe, PayPal, Square, etc.)
- Ensure your payment processor is **PCI-DSS compliant**
- Display trust signals (SSL padlock, payment logos)
- If using Stripe or similar, they handle PCI compliance — but you must still follow their integration requirements

### VAT Rules for Online Sales
- If your turnover exceeds **€37,500** (services) or **€75,000** (goods), you must register for VAT in Ireland
- Standard VAT rate in Ireland: **23%**
- Reduced rates: 13.5% (some services/construction), 9% (hospitality/newspapers), 4.8% (agriculture)
- Selling **digital services to EU consumers**: you must charge VAT at the **buyer's country rate** — use the **OSS (One Stop Shop)** system via Revenue to manage this
- Selling to businesses (B2B) across the EU: reverse charge rules apply

## 4. Accessibility — Full Requirements

### Why It Matters Now
The **European Accessibility Act (EAA)** requires private sector websites and apps to meet accessibility standards if they provide services in the EU. The deadline for compliance is **28 June 2025** for new products/services. Existing services get until **28 June 2030** in some cases.

### WCAG 2.1 Level AA — The Standard You Must Meet

**Perceivable**
- All images must have **alt text** (or empty alt="" if decorative)
- Videos need **captions** and ideally transcripts
- Audio needs transcripts
- Text must have a **contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1** against the background (3:1 for large text)
- Content must be **resizable up to 200%** without loss of functionality
- Don't rely on colour alone to convey information

**Operable**
- All functionality must work with a **keyboard alone** (no mouse required)
- Users must be able to **pause, stop, or hide** any moving, blinking, or auto-updating content
- No content that flashes more than **3 times per second** (seizure risk)
- Each page must have a **descriptive title**
- Navigation links must be consistent across pages
- Provide **skip navigation links** (to jump to main content)
- Give users enough time to read and interact — don't time out sessions without warning

**Understandable**
- Declare the **language of the page** in the HTML (`<html lang="en">`)
- Navigation must be **consistent** throughout the site
- Form fields must have proper **labels**
- Error messages must clearly explain what went wrong and how to fix it

**Robust**
- Use valid, semantic HTML
- Ensure compatibility with **screen readers** (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- Use ARIA attributes where necessary

### Accessibility Statement (Required for Public Sector, Best Practice for All)
- State your compliance level
- List any known issues and planned fix dates
- Provide a contact method for accessibility issues
- Reference the enforcement body

### Tools to Test Accessibility
- **WAVE** (wave.webaim.org) — free browser tool
- **axe DevTools** — Chrome extension
- **Lighthouse** in Chrome DevTools
- **Colour Contrast Analyser** — desktop app
- Manual keyboard testing — tab through your entire site

## 5. Security — Full Technical Checklist

### SSL/HTTPS
- Every website must use HTTPS — get a free certificate via **Let's Encrypt** or buy one from your host
- Redirect all HTTP traffic to HTTPS (301 redirect)
- Use **HSTS** (HTTP Strict Transport Security) headers
- Ensure your SSL certificate covers all subdomains if needed (wildcard cert)

### HTTP Security Headers (Add to Your Server Config)
```
Content-Security-Policy (CSP)
X-Frame-Options: DENY or SAMEORIGIN
X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff
Referrer-Policy: strict-origin-when-cross-origin
Permissions-Policy
Strict-Transport-Security (HSTS)
```
Test your headers at **securityheaders.com**

### Password & Account Security
- Admin passwords must be **strong and unique** (use a password manager)
- Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all admin accounts
- Limit login attempts (brute force protection)
- Change default admin usernames (e.g. never use "admin" on WordPress)
- Use **role-based access** — don't give everyone admin access

### Platform Security (WordPress etc.)
- Keep the CMS, themes, and plugins **updated at all times**
- Delete unused plugins and themes
- Use a security plugin (Wordfence, Sucuri)
- Set up **automated backups** — daily, stored off-site
- Consider a **Web Application Firewall (WAF)**
- Disable XML-RPC if you're not using it
- Hide your WordPress version number

### General Infrastructure
- Use a reputable, **EU-based host** (compliance + performance)
- Set up **DDoS protection** (Cloudflare free tier covers this)
- Run regular **vulnerability scans**
- Use **Content Delivery Network (CDN)** for performance and security
- Monitor uptime with free tools (UptimeRobot, Better Uptime)

## 6. SEO — Full Deep Dive

### Core Web Vitals (Google's Ranking Signals)
| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint) | Loading speed of main content | Under 2.5 seconds |
| **INP** (Interaction to Next Paint) | Responsiveness to user input | Under 200ms |
| **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift) | Visual stability (elements jumping around) | Under 0.1 |

Test with: **PageSpeed Insights** (pagespeed.web.dev), **Google Search Console**, or **GTmetrix**

### Technical SEO Checklist
- **robots.txt** — tells search engines what to crawl (don't accidentally block your whole site)
- **sitemap.xml** — submit to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools
- **Canonical tags** — prevent duplicate content issues
- **Hreflang tags** — if serving Irish English vs British English vs international audiences
- **Structured data / Schema markup** — helps Google understand your content
- **404 pages** — create a helpful custom 404 page
- **Redirect chains** — avoid long chains (301 → 301 → 301); go direct
- **Crawl budget** — don't waste it on low-value pages (thank-you pages, filters, etc.)
- **Core Web Vitals** — see above
- **Mobile-first design** — Google indexes the mobile version of your site

### Schema Markup Types Relevant to Irish Businesses
- `LocalBusiness` — address, hours, phone, area served
- `Product` — for e-commerce
- `FAQPage` — FAQ sections
- `BreadcrumbList` — navigation trail
- `Review` / `AggregateRating` — product/service reviews
- `Event` — if you run events
- `Article` / `BlogPosting` — for content

Use **Google's Rich Results Test** to validate your schema.

### On-Page SEO
- One **H1 per page** — make it descriptive and include your target keyword naturally
- Title tags: **50–60 characters**, unique per page, include keyword near the start
- Meta descriptions: **150–160 characters**, written to encourage clicks (not a ranking factor, but affects click-through rate)
- **URL structure**: `/services/web-design-dublin/` not `/page?id=47`
- **Internal linking**: link related pages together with descriptive anchor text
- **Image optimisation**: compress images (use WebP format), add alt text, use descriptive filenames
- Avoid **keyword stuffing** — write naturally for humans first
- Aim for **E-E-A-T**: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — Google rewards this heavily

### Local SEO for Ireland — Full Checklist
- Claim and fully complete your **Google Business Profile**
  - Add all categories
  - Upload photos regularly
  - Add your opening hours (including Irish bank holidays)
  - Enable messaging
  - Post updates regularly
  - Answer questions in the Q&A section
- Respond to **every Google review** — positive and negative
- Build **local citations** (consistent NAP across):
  - Golden Pages (goldenpages.ie)
  - Yelp Ireland
  - Bing Places
  - Apple Maps Connect
  - Foursquare
  - Hotfrog
  - IrishBusinesses.ie
  - Local county directories
- Get **local backlinks**:
  - Local newspapers (IrishTimes.com, TheJournal.ie, local papers)
  - Chamber of Commerce membership listings
  - Local business association directories
  - Sponsor local events for links
- Target **county and city-specific keywords** (Dublin, Cork, Galway, Limerick, etc.)
- Create **location pages** if you serve multiple areas

### Content Strategy for Irish SEO
- Target **long-tail Irish keywords**: "solicitor Galway city centre", "wedding photographer Wicklow"
- Create content around **Irish search intent**: Irish-specific guides, comparisons, "best of" lists
- Write about **local topics** relevant to your industry
- Use **Irish events and dates** for content hooks (St. Patrick's Day, Bloomsday, All-Ireland finals, etc.)
- Consider writing about **Irish regulations** relevant to your industry — high-value informational content with very low competition

## 7. Performance Optimisation

### Image Optimisation
- Use **WebP** format (significantly smaller than JPEG/PNG)
- **Compress** all images before uploading (TinyPNG, Squoosh)
- Set proper **width and height attributes** to prevent layout shift (CLS)
- Use **lazy loading** for images below the fold (`loading="lazy"`)
- Use a **CDN** to serve images from servers closer to the user

### Code Optimisation
- **Minify** HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
- **Defer or async** non-critical JavaScript
- Remove unused CSS (PurgeCSS for Tailwind etc.)
- Reduce third-party scripts — every external script adds load time
- Use **browser caching** — set cache headers for static assets
- Enable **Gzip or Brotli compression** on your server
- Use **preload** for critical fonts and resources

### Hosting for Ireland
- If most of your users are in Ireland, use a host with **EU data centres** — ideally in Dublin, Frankfurt, or Amsterdam (AWS eu-west-1 Dublin is ideal)
- Good EU-based hosts: **Kinsta**, **WP Engine** (EU region), **Siteground** (EU servers), **Blacknight** (Irish company), **WebHosting.ie** (Irish)
- Use **Cloudflare** (free CDN) to serve cached content from edge locations worldwide

## 8. Domain & Brand

### Getting a .ie Domain
- Managed by **IEDR (iedr.ie)**
- To register, you must prove an Irish connection:
  - Irish resident (with Irish address)
  - Business registered in Ireland (CRO number)
  - Irish trademark holder
  - Association with Ireland
- Cost: approximately **€25–€35/year** depending on registrar
- Recommended Irish registrars: **Blacknight**, **Register365**, **WebHosting.ie**
- A .ie domain signals trust to Irish users and **improves local SEO rankings** in Ireland

### Trademark Considerations
- Before launching, check your name isn't already trademarked at **ipoi.gov.ie**
- Also check the **EU Intellectual Property Office (euipo.europa.eu)** for EU-wide trademarks
- Consider registering your own trademark — costs approximately €70–€170 per class via IPOI
- Check domain and social media handle availability before committing to a name

## 9. Legal Pages — Full Templates Guide

### Privacy Policy Must Include
Every item listed in Section 1 above. Keep it in **plain English** — legalese is not a GDPR requirement and actually works against you.

### Terms & Conditions Should Cover
- Who the contract is between
- Acceptable use of the site
- Intellectual property ownership
- Limitation of liability
- Governing law (**Republic of Ireland** and **EU law**)
- Dispute resolution
- How you can change the T&Cs
- Account suspension/termination (if you have accounts)

### Returns Policy Must State
- Time period for returns (minimum 14 days by law)
- Condition goods must be in
- Who pays return shipping
- Refund timeline (must be within 14 days of receiving the return)
- Any non-returnable items (clearly flagged **before** purchase)

### Acceptable Use Policy (If You Have User Accounts or UGC)
- What users can and cannot post
- Your right to remove content
- Consequences of violations
- How to report content

## 11. Ongoing Compliance — What to Do Regularly

### Monthly

- Update your cookie audit (have any new third-party tools been added?)
